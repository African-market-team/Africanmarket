Our story

Sauti East Africa is a women-led social enterprise based in Kenya combining development research and technology solutions to address the digital information gap facing women in trade.

We build, implement, and scale mobile trade and market information solutions for small and medium enterprises (SMEs) across East Africa. Since 2017, we have directly reached over 14,000 business operators in “low technology” environments in Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, and Tanzania and indirectly empowered many more traders as partners in program design and technical implementation.

Sauti East Africa brings proven expertise in information technology solutions, research, mobile data collection, evaluation, and trade insights.

Our Mission

To address information barriers in “low technology” environments through scalable and sustainable technology and research solutions.

Our Impact

WE EMPOWER MSMES TO ACCESS ACCURATE, UP-TO-DATE, AND OFFICIALLY SOURCED INFORMATION USING MOBILE PHONES Sauti’s mobile trade and market information platform uses SMS, USSD, and WhatsApp technology to close the digital information divide for women in trade across East Africa. Project implementers and researchers can also download data on women-led enterprises through Sauti’s interactive trade insights data dashboard.

Our Approach

Sauti’s mobile trade and market information platform uses SMS, USSD, and WhatsApp technology to close the digital information divide for women in trade across East Africa.

Project implementers and researchers can also download data on women-led enterprises through Sauti’s interactive trade insights data dashboard. We help projects to integrate flexible and cost-effective information technologies into design and delivery.

Our Reach

In 2017 Sauti launched our mobile-based trade and market information platform in Kenya, and Uganda, and we have since expanded to Rwanda and Tanzania with more than 13,000 users to-date. We have also conducted research assignments for clients such as Google.org, TradeMark East Africa, Busara Centre for Behavioural Economics, and Tetra Tech across six African countries to support them in their research and development work.
